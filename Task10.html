Task 10

Cylindrical mirror anamorphosis — mapping to a circular fan

Overview

Generate a printable “fan” that reconstructs an image in a polished cylindrical mirror. The program fits your picture inside a unit circle and maps those pixels to an arc of radius Rf below the circle. Print the fan and place a shiny cylinder at its centre to see the image appear upright in the reflection.

Key Theory

A vertical cylinder behaves like a 2D circular mirror in cross‑section. Rays reflect with equal angles about the local normal. We compute, for each point (X, Y) inside the circle, where its reflected ray intersects a plane (the printed sheet). That point becomes the location for the pixel in the “fan.” Restricting the azimuth to a sector of angle Δφ creates a compact wedge that’s easy to print.

  • Mirror radius R (blue circle) with the picture fitted inside it.
  • Fan parameters: sector angle (ARC_DEG), maximum radius factor (RADIUS_FACTOR), and orientation (below/above).
  • Mapping: The code uses an inverse solve with bilinear sampling for smooth output, or a forward “splat” mode for debugging.

To view the anamorph: print the fan at 100%, cut around the wedge if you like, and place a polished cylinder (or chrome tube) centred on the fan’s origin. The reflection reconstructs the original image.
