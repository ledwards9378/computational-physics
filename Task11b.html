Task 11b

Rainbow turning angles εmin vs frequency (dispersion-coloured)

Overview

Compute the deflection angles at the stationary points (the bright “rainbow angles”) for the primary and secondary bows across the visible spectrum, and plot εmin vs frequency. Points are coloured by wavelength to show the spectral ordering.

Key Theory

For each refractive index n(λ), the incident angle θ⋆ that extremizes ε(θ) satisfies closed forms:

  • Primary: θ⋆ = sin⁻¹(√((4 − n²)/3)), εmin = 4r − 2θ with r = sin⁻¹(sin θ⋆/n)
  • Secondary: θ⋆ = sin⁻¹(√((9 − n²)/8)), εmin = π − 6r + 2θ

The script sweeps frequency f (THz), evaluates n(f) via the empirical model (n² − 1)⁻² = 1.731 − 0.261 (f/10¹⁵ Hz)², then computes θ⋆ and εmin for both orders. The result shows primary angles ≈ 41–43° and secondary ≈ 50–53°, with slight dispersion.
