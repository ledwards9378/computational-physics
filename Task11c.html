Task 11c

Internal refraction angle φ at the rainbow vs frequency

Overview

Plot the internal refraction angle φ = r(θ⋆, n) at the primary and secondary rainbow turning points as a function of frequency, together with the critical angle φc = sin⁻¹(1/n). Points are coloured by wavelength.

Key Theory

The stationary incidence angles are θ⋆(n) from Task 11b. The internal refraction angles at those points are simply r = sin⁻¹(sin θ⋆/n). Comparing r to the critical angle φc = sin⁻¹(1/n) explains why internal reflections are lossy: r is smaller than φc across the visible, so some light exits at each reflection.
