Task 12a

Dynamic prism — white light through a triangular prism

Overview

Interactively trace a beam of white light through an isosceles glass prism. Use sliders to vary the apex angle α and the angle of incidence θi; the program splits the beam by wavelength using BK7 Sellmeier dispersion and draws the incident ray, the internal path, and the dispersed outgoing bundle.

Key Theory

For each wavelength (hence refractive index n(λ)) the ray refracts at each face according to Snell’s law. In an isosceles prism with apex angle α the interior refraction angles satisfy r1 + r2 = α, with r1 = sin⁻¹(sin θi / n) and the emergence angle obeys sin θt = n sin r2 (provided there’s no total internal reflection at the exit).

Eliminating r1, r2 gives the standard transmission formula used in the graphs: sin θt = √(n² − sin² θi) sin α − sin θi cos α. The overall deflection of the white light is δ = θi + θt − α. Because n(λ) varies slightly with wavelength, different colours exit at slightly different angles → a spectrum.
