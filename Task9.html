Task 9

Convex spherical mirror — virtual image formation

Overview

Interactively explore how a convex spherical mirror forms a smaller, upright virtual image behind the mirror. Move the object with the arrow keys and watch the ray diagram and the theoretical (paraxial) prediction update.

Key Theory

For a spherical mirror of radius R, the focal length is f = −R/2 for a convex mirror (negative because the image is virtual and appears behind the mirror). Using the mirror equation 1/u + 1/v = 1/f with u the object distance from the vertex and v the image distance (negative for a virtual image), we get a magnification m = −v/u which is positive and less than 1, so the virtual image is upright and smaller.

  • Ray 1: from the object horizontally to the mirror, reflecting as if it came from the focal point.
  • Ray 2: towards the center; the reflected ray obeys the law of reflection about the surface normal.
  • Their backward extensions meet at the virtual image point (behind the mirror).
